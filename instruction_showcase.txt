Showcase Screen Concept: The Raceboard Chase (Kinetic Kinetic-Friction & Shuttle Flight Physics)
The Concept: Your items do not sit idle in a static grid. The entire canvas is a highly tensioned raceboard of an industrial power loom. The UI is driven by an advanced physics engine simulating Kinetic Friction, Sudden Acceleration, and Tensioned Interlocking. Your collection—rendered as iron-tipped persimmon-wood fly-shuttles, polished silk-weaving shuttles, and mechanical pirns—rests inside the "shuttle boxes" on either side of the screen. The background is not a flat canvas; it is a dense, vibrating layer of thousands of vertical warp threads that split and form a open "shed" dynamically as the shuttles fly across them.
The Interaction (The Magic): You do not scroll; you pick and hurl the shuttles through the shed.
•	Swiping horizontally across the screen acts as a powerful "picker stick" strike. You physically hurl a shuttle node from one side of the device to the other. The node shoots across the warp mesh with incredible velocity, leaving a trailing visual blur of interlocking threads.
•	The device's haptic motor delivers a sharp, explosive Thwack! at the moment of launch, followed by a high-frequency, textured buzz as the iron tips slide frictionally across the wooden raceboard, ending in a heavy mechanical Clack! as it slams into the opposite box.
•	The weight of the wood matters: A heavy, iron-tipped wool-shuttle node takes significant force to launch, moving with a deep, low-frequency rumble that visibly shakes the entire warp grid. A delicate silk-weaving shuttle shoots across with a light, razor-sharp haptic hiss.
•	Tapping a shuttle while it is resting in the box causes it to "eject" its internal pirn or bobbin. The pirn spins out with a satisfying spiral animation and a light, ratcheting haptic click, allowing you to examine the thread density.
The Visuals: A palette drawn from the humid, oil-scented air of a New England textile mill: Persimmon Wood Gold (#C88E46), Loom Iron Black (#1B1A19), Indigo Warp (#1E2E3D), and Raw Cotton (#F6F4EE). The UI leans into a rustic, industrial bento-box aesthetic. The shuttle nodes are modeled with breathtaking precision: showing the oily, hand-rubbed sheen of decades of lanolin exposure, micro-pitting on the forged iron tips, and the subtle grain of the persimmon wood. The background threads continuously vibrate at a high frequency, blurring slightly to simulate a loom operating at full production speed.
The Focus Transition (The Shed Lock Inspection): To inspect an item, you must perform the critical mechanical action of the weaver: Dropping the Pick.
You drag the moving shuttle node into the dead center of the raceboard. Instead of letting it pass, you perform a sudden two-finger pinch gesture, which simulates the loom's "reed" slamming forward to beat the weft thread into the cloth.
The entire vertical warp grid instantly collapses around the shuttle with a violent, definitive haptic CRUNCH. The motion of the loom freezes entirely. The background threads weave themselves together, forming a tight, solid indigo fabric canvas that serves as a backdrop. The Focus Panel then slides out like a smooth wooden weaver's bench, displaying the instrument type, manufacturer, weight, era, and the specific steel mill, foundry, or ceramic kiln the specimen once calibrated, paired with a stunning macro photograph capturing the stamp of the foundry that forged its iron tips.
Why this works perfectly: The beauty of a fly-shuttle is completely defined by its blinding speed, its kinetic energy, and its violent, rhythmic impact. By turning the screen into a live power-loom raceboard and requiring the user to physically strike, catch, and mechanically lock their shuttles to access the archive, you perfectly capture the deafening, high-speed romance of the industrial weaving heritage.
