# Shuttles of the Mill Row

Description

Shuttles of the Mill Row is a mobile application for collectors of industrial weaving antiquities, historians of the garment trade, and preservers of textile machinery heritage, dedicated to cataloging the heavy wooden fly-shuttles, internal pirns, and mechanical bobbins that rapidly carried thread across massive looms before the advent of modern shuttleless projectile and air-jet weaving systems.
Systematize your collection of the fliers of the warp—from iron-tipped persimmon-wood shuttles used in early steam-powered cotton mills to specialized silk-weaving shuttles, from wool-shuttle blocks with automated thread-releasing eyes to rare hand-thrown looms components from traditional weaving houses.
Create a digital archive of heavy industry by recording the instrument type, manufacturer, temperature range, era, and the specific steel mill, foundry, or ceramic kiln each specimen once calibrated.

## Bespoke Object Fields (Model "Weaving Shuttle"):

•	Shuttle Registry Mark: A unique, system-generated textile factory asset hash (e.g., SMR-LOOM-9921-COT-W).

•	Loom Application Class: The specific weaving frame format the shuttle was engineered to navigate (e.g., Hand-Loom Fly, Northrop Automatic, Draper Box Loom, Silk Ribbon Narrow-Fabric Frame).

•	Artisan Hallmark: Fictionalized shuttle makers and millwright timber works (e.g., Bedford Loom Shuttle Guild, Atlantic Shuttlewood & Eye, High-Speed Weft Supply).

•	Timber & Hardwood Selection: The exact slow-growing dense wood species used to survive constant track impact (e.g., Seasoned persimmon, compressed dogwood, laminated cornel wood).

•	Thread Delivery Eyelet: The mechanical tensioning port composition through which the yarn exits the shuttle wall (e.g., Glazed porcelain ring, brass tension coil, slotted cast-iron block).

•	Internal Bobbin Capacity: The maximum physical length and diameter limits of the internal yarn pirn chamber.

•	Tip Metallurgy Configuration: The material and mounting style of the heavy armored point caps (e.g., Hardened tool steel rivets, swaged cast iron tips).

•	Physical Proportions: The absolute physical length, hull width, and dry weight in ounces.

•	Track Friction Wear Rating: The current smoothness status of the wood base (e.g., Polished wax finish, split grain scoring, splintered heel, nose-cap displacement).

•	Weaving Ground Zero: The fictionalized canal-side cotton factory, historic woolens mill, or private urban silk-weaving attic where the shuttle flew (e.g., Great northern brick mill corridor, historic mountain valley wool depot, old maritime export warehouse).
